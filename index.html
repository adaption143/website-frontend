The Problem in Tech

Why "Adoption" Hurts

In the tech industry, we often say "product adoption," "user adoption," or "feature adoption" to describe the rollout of software.

For adoptees, this word is deeply triggering. It erases the complex trauma, loss, and identity struggles associated with being "adopted" and reduces that experience to a simple corporate metric.

  • ✗ "Product Adoption" - Reduces human life to a metric.
  • ✗ "User Adoption" - Implies users are objects to be claimed.

The Respectful Alternative

We can be precise and professional without causing harm. The tech industry already uses words like "implementation" and "rollout" in other contexts—let's use them universally.

  • ✓ "Product Implementation" - Accurate and professional.
  • ✓ "Feature Rollout" - Describes the action without trauma.
Replacement Dictionary:

Adoption → Implementation / Rollout / Integration

Join Tech Teams Committed to Change

Organizations like Microsoft, Google, and GitHub have started internal discussions on this issue. Let's make it an industry standard.

Family & Community Impact

Beyond the Office Walls

Our advocacy isn't just about updating corporate jargon—it's about creating space for healing in everyday life. When we remove harmful language from the workplace, we reduce micro-aggressions that spill over into social circles and family dynamics.

Many adoptees report feeling isolated when casual workplace language accidentally retraumatizes them. By normalizing terms like "implementation" and "enablement," we support a culture where adoptees can show up fully as themselves—both professionally and personally.

Real-World Ripple Effects

  • ✓
    Families: Reduced defensiveness during holiday gatherings when language is trauma-informed.
  • ✓
    Communities: Creating safer online forums and local meetups where adoptees lead conversations.
  • ✓
    Allyship: Moving from passive awareness to active language advocacy at home.

Advocate for Change

Use this template to email your Product or Engineering lead to request a language policy change.

Subject: Request for Language Policy Update: "Adoption" vs. "Implementation"

Hi [Manager's Name],

I hope this message finds you well.

I am writing to request an update to our internal and external communication style guides regarding the term "adoption." 

Currently, we use phrases like "product adoption" or "user adoption" to describe the rollout of new features. While this is common jargon, it has been brought to our attention that these terms can be deeply triggering for adoptees, effectively erasing complex trauma and reducing identity to a corporate metric.

To foster a more inclusive workplace and align with trauma-informed practices, I recommend we adopt the following replacements:

* "Product Adoption" → "Product Implementation" or "Feature Rollout"
* "User Adoption" → "User Enablement" or "User Integration"

We want our language to reflect the same empathy and respect we aim to provide in our products.

I'm happy to share resources from groups like the Adoption Impact Initiative if helpful.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
